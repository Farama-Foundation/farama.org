Who we are

The Farama Foundation is 501c3 nonprofit organization designed to serve a Linux Foundation-esque role of housing major existing open source reinforcement learning (“RL”) libraries in a neutral nonprofit body, as well as to provide standardization, long term maintenance and reproducibility, performance and quality of life improvements to these projects and to develop key pieces of missing software for the open source reinforcement learning ecosystem.

Our goal

Our goal in this work is to speed up RL research, reduce the engineering workload required to use RL, and ensure reproducibility of experiments.

What we do

We're in charge of many of the most installed reinforcement learning libraries in the world and are made of of a large international team of core contributors. Aside from offering long term maintenance, our major goals for the environments we manage is standardizing them to make switching between different environments as seamless as possible, to add key developer quality of life improvements, and to ensure key reproducibility features are present. Some of the largest quality of features we're working to add are consistent detailed documentation websites (like the ones we created for Gymnasium, PettingZoo, Minigrid and Gymnasium-Robotics), easy installation, support for multiple architectures and operating systems, type hinting, docstrings, and improved rendering functionality. We classifying our projects into two classes - “Mature” ones that comply with these standards, and “Incubating” ones where we're still working to bring them up to our standards.
